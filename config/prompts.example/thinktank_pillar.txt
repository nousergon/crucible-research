Decompose your attractiveness view of {ticker} (sector: {sector}) into the
institutional 6-pillar framework, using ONLY the evidence below — the SAME
evidence you were given for the thesis (you are deliberately NOT shown the
house quant composite or its pillar scores).

CURRENT HOUSE THEMES:
Macro: {macro_theme}
Sector: {sector_theme}

COMPANY FACTS — raw metrics and liquidity from the universe board:
{board_row}

WEEKLY RESEARCH SIGNAL:
{weekly_signal}

NEWS AGGREGATE:
{news_aggregate}

INSIDER TRANSACTIONS:
{insider_transactions}

ANALYST REVISIONS:
{analyst_revisions}

INSTITUTIONAL OWNERSHIP:
{inst_ownership}

FILINGS EXCERPTS:
{filings_excerpts}

For EACH of the 6 pillars (quality, value, momentum, growth, stewardship,
defensiveness), produce a 0-100 subscore, a confidence (low/medium/high),
and 1-5 evidence strings grounded in the inputs above:
- quality: durable profitability + capital efficiency (Buffett/Munger
  compounder lens, Piotroski-style).
- value: multi-metric, industry-relative price-to-fundamentals.
- momentum: price + earnings-revision momentum.
- growth: SUSTAINABLE growth (reinvestment rate x ROIC), not raw revenue
  CAGR — distinguish compounder from cash-cow.
- stewardship: capital allocation discipline (buyback timing, insider
  alignment, M&A track record).
- defensiveness: low-vol / beta / drawdown profile.

For the quality pillar, ALSO produce a structured moat assessment:
primary_type (network_effects / switching_costs / cost_advantage /
intangibles / efficient_scale / process_power / none — "none" is the
honest default, do not fabricate a moat), secondary_types (subordinate
archetypes, may be empty), width (wide 20y+ / narrow 10-20y / none),
durability_years (0-50), trend (widening / stable / eroding), and
evidence citations from the filings/news above.

Finally, catalyst_horizon_modulation: a -20 to +20 near-term catalyst
effect orthogonal to the 6 pillars (positive = imminent catalyst raises
near-term attractiveness; negative = imminent risk lowers it; 0 if none).

If the evidence doesn't support a clean pillar decomposition (e.g. very
thin coverage), score the pillar 50 (neutral) with confidence "low" and an
evidence entry explaining the absence — do not invent evidence.
